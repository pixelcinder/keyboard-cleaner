How it works

Four steps, zero accidents

No configuration, no fuss. One tap is all it takes to get a fully locked keyboard ready for a deep clean.

🔐
Beta — first launch only: the app is not yet code-signed, so macOS Gatekeeper will block it on first open. Right-click the app and choose Open, then confirm. Apple's guide →
1

Grant Accessibility Access

On first launch, grant one-time Accessibility permission in System Settings. This is how the app intercepts keystrokes before they reach other apps — nothing else is accessed.

2

Tap Lock

Hit the Lock button or press ⌃⌘L from anywhere on your Mac. Every key on your keyboard is immediately blocked.

3

Clean freely

Scrub, wipe, and press every key as hard as you like. Nothing gets through — no accidental inputs, no opened apps, no typed text.

4

Unlock with Touch ID

Place your finger on Touch ID when you're done. Or use the on-screen PIN pad with your mouse — no keyboard needed.

Features

Everything you need,
nothing you don't

Built specifically for macOS — native, lightweight, and designed to get out of your way.

🔒

Total keyboard block

Every keystroke — letters, shortcuts, function keys — is intercepted before it reaches any app. System-wide and instant.

👆

Touch ID unlock

The fastest way back. A single finger touch on the Touch ID sensor unlocks your keyboard in under a second.

🔢

PIN fallback

Set a 4-digit PIN as a backup. The on-screen numpad is mouse-clickable, so you're never stuck even if Touch ID fails.

⏱

Auto-unlock timer

Set a countdown — 1, 2, 5, 10, or 30 minutes. A live ring shows the time remaining, and the keyboard unlocks automatically when it hits zero.

🖥

Full-screen overlay

A beautiful full-screen cover keeps you from accidentally clicking anything while you clean. Covers all displays at once.

☰

Menu bar control

Lock your keyboard directly from the menu bar without switching apps. The icon reflects your current lock state at a glance.
